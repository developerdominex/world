Thermodynamics and Heat - Physics

Subtopics of Thermodynamics and Heat with Key Formulas
Subtopic Main Concepts / Equations
Temperature and Heat Heat: Q = mcΔT
Specific heat: c = Q/(mΔT)
Thermal expansion (linear): ΔL = αLΔT
Thermal expansion (volume): ΔV = βVΔT
Laws of Thermodynamics Zeroth Law: Thermal equilibrium concept
First Law: ΔU = Q − W
Second Law: ΔS ≥ 0, efficiency η = W/Q_h ≤ 1
Third Law: S → 0 as T → 0
Heat Transfer Conduction: Q = kA(ΔT/d)t
Convection: Heat transfer by fluid motion
Radiation: P = σA(T⁴ − T₀⁴), Stefan-Boltzmann law
Work and Energy in Thermodynamic Systems Work: W = PΔV
Internal energy (ideal gas): U = (3/2)nRT (monatomic)
Enthalpy: H = U + PV
Kinetic Theory of Gases Pressure: P = (1/3) ρ v_rms²
Ideal gas law: PV = nRT
Root mean square speed: v_rms = √(3RT/M)
Heat Engines and Refrigerators Carnot engine efficiency: η = 1 − T_c/T_h
Coefficient of performance (refrigerator): COP = Q_c/W
Phase Changes Latent heat: Q = mL (fusion, vaporization, sublimation)
Phase diagrams: Shows state of substance with T and P
Critical point: Substance cannot exist as liquid above this T and P
