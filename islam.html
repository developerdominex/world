Islam

Islam is a major world religion founded in the 7th century CE, based on the teachings of the Prophet Muhammad. It emphasizes submission to God, moral living, and community.

History

Islam began in the early 7th century in Mecca, present-day Saudi Arabia, with the revelations received by the Prophet Muhammad from Allah (God). These revelations were later compiled into the Qur’an, the holy book of Islam. Muhammad's teachings spread throughout the Arabian Peninsula and, after his death, expanded rapidly across Asia, Africa, and Europe, forming a major religious, cultural, and political civilization.

Core Beliefs

Islam centers on the belief in one God (Allah) and the guidance provided by prophets, with Muhammad being the final prophet. Key principles include faith, prayer, charity, fasting, and pilgrimage. Muslims follow the Qur’an and Hadith (sayings of Muhammad) as sources of spiritual and moral guidance.

  • Belief in the Oneness of God (Tawhid).
  • Belief in prophets, including Adam, Moses, Jesus, and Muhammad as the final prophet.
  • Belief in angels, divine books, and the Day of Judgment.
  • Living a life according to divine guidance, including ethics, morality, and social responsibility.

Practices (The Five Pillars)

The Five Pillars of Islam are the foundation of a Muslim's faith and practice:

  • Shahada: Declaration of faith – "There is no god but Allah, and Muhammad is His messenger."
  • Salat: Performing ritual prayers five times a day facing Mecca.
  • Zakat: Giving alms or charity to help those in need.
  • Sawm: Fasting during the month of Ramadan as a form of self-purification and empathy.
  • Hajj: Pilgrimage to Mecca, required once in a lifetime if physically and financially able.

Branches of Islam

Islam has several major branches, reflecting differences in interpretation and practice:

  • Sunni Islam: The largest branch, emphasizing the community’s consensus and the example of the Prophet.
  • Shia Islam: Focuses on leadership through the family of the Prophet Muhammad, especially Ali and his descendants.
  • Sufism: A mystical dimension of Islam that emphasizes spiritual experience, meditation, and inner purification.

Influence

Islam has shaped cultures, art, science, and governance across the world. Muslim civilizations contributed to mathematics, medicine, astronomy, architecture, and literature. Islamic values continue to guide daily life, community practices, and global ethics for millions of people today.
