Reasoning Through Language Arts (RLA)

The RLA section of the GED tests your ability to read, understand, analyze, and communicate effectively. It combines reading comprehension, grammar, and essay writing.

Reading Comprehension

This part tests your ability to read passages from fiction (stories, literature) and nonfiction (articles, essays, reports). Key skills include:

  • Identifying Main Ideas: Understanding the central point or argument of a passage.
  • Finding Supporting Details: Recognizing facts, examples, or reasons that support the main idea.
  • Recognizing Themes: Understanding the broader message or moral of a story.
  • Drawing Inferences: Making logical conclusions from information that isn’t stated directly.
  • Understanding Vocabulary in Context: Determining the meaning of words based on usage.

Practice Tip: Read short passages, summarize the main idea, and highlight supporting details.

Writing

This part evaluates your ability to write clearly and correctly, focusing on grammar, sentence structure, punctuation, and clarity. Key skills include:

  • Grammar and Punctuation: Use commas, periods, apostrophes, and proper sentence structure.
  • Sentence Structure: Avoid run-on sentences and fragments.
  • Paragraph Coherence: Organize ideas logically so sentences flow naturally.
  • Clarity and Style: Write precise and easily understood sentences.

Practice Tip: Write short paragraphs, edit for clarity, and correct grammar errors.

Extended Response (Essay)

You will write a short essay (200–300 words) responding to a prompt. This can include:

  • Analyzing a text: Explain how the author supports their argument.
  • Constructing an argument: Present your own opinion with evidence.

Key skills for the essay:

  • Reading the Prompt Carefully: Make sure you address exactly what is asked.
  • Organizing Your Essay: Introduction with thesis, body paragraphs with evidence, conclusion summarizing main points.
  • Using Evidence: Support ideas with examples from the text or logical reasoning.
  • Grammar and Clarity: Write clear, correct sentences.

Practice Tip: Outline your essay before writing, practice timed essays, and review grammar rules.

Summary: The RLA section measures your ability to read, understand, and analyze text while communicating your ideas clearly. Focus on reading comprehension, grammar, and essay writing skills.

Mathematical Reasoning

The Mathematical Reasoning section of the GED tests your ability to work with numbers, solve problems, and analyze data. It covers basic arithmetic, algebra, geometry, measurement, and data interpretation.

Basic Math

This includes arithmetic operations, fractions, decimals, percentages, ratios, and estimation. Key skills:

  • Adding, subtracting, multiplying, and dividing whole numbers, fractions, and decimals.
  • Converting fractions to decimals and percentages, and vice versa.
  • Solving ratio and proportion problems.
  • Estimating answers to check reasonableness.

Practice Tip: Work on mental math, simplify fractions, and practice percentage problems regularly.

Algebra

Algebra focuses on solving equations, inequalities, working with variables, and applying functions. Key skills:

  • Solving linear equations and inequalities with one or more variables.
  • Understanding expressions, exponents, and algebraic formulas.
  • Applying algebra to real-world word problems.
  • Recognizing patterns and functions.

Practice Tip: Practice solving for variables, translating word problems into equations, and checking solutions.

Geometry and Measurement

This area tests understanding of shapes, sizes, and spatial relationships. Key skills:

  • Calculating perimeter, area, and volume for various shapes (squares, rectangles, triangles, circles, prisms, cylinders).
  • Understanding angles, including complementary, supplementary, and triangle properties.
  • Using measurement units and converting between them.
  • Applying geometric formulas to solve practical problems.

Practice Tip: Draw diagrams for word problems and memorize basic area and volume formulas.

Data Analysis

This part measures your ability to interpret and analyze data from graphs, tables, and charts. Key skills:

  • Reading bar graphs, line graphs, pie charts, and tables accurately.
  • Understanding probability and basic statistics, including mean, median, mode, and range.
  • Solving real-world problems using data interpretation.
  • Recognizing trends and making predictions based on data.

Practice Tip: Practice reading charts quickly, calculating averages, and applying probability to simple scenarios.

Summary: The GED Mathematical Reasoning section tests arithmetic, algebra, geometry, measurement, and data skills. Focus on understanding concepts, applying formulas, and solving word problems efficiently to succeed.

Science

The Science section of the GED evaluates your ability to understand scientific concepts, interpret data, and apply reasoning skills to real-world situations. It covers Life Science, Physical Science, Earth and Space Science, and Scientific Reasoning.

Life Science

Life Science focuses on living organisms, ecosystems, and biological processes. Key skills include:

  • Cells and Genetics: Understanding cell structure, functions, DNA, and inheritance.
  • Human Body Systems: Learn how organs and systems work together to maintain health.
  • Ecology and Ecosystems: Study interactions between organisms and their environment.
  • Evolution and Adaptation: Understand how species change over time to survive in their habitats.

Practice Tip: Review diagrams of cells and body systems, and practice explaining ecological relationships.

Physical Science

Physical Science explores the properties of matter, energy, and the forces that govern the universe. Key skills include:

  • Motion and Forces: Newton’s laws, speed, velocity, and acceleration.
  • Energy and Waves: Types of energy, energy transfer, sound and light waves.
  • Matter and Atoms: States of matter, atomic structure, and chemical bonding.
  • Chemical Reactions: Understand reactants, products, and balancing equations.

Practice Tip: Solve simple physics and chemistry problems and visualize experiments conceptually.

Earth and Space Science

This area covers the Earth, its environment, and the universe. Key skills include:

  • Geology: Rocks, minerals, Earth’s layers, plate tectonics.
  • Meteorology: Weather, climate, atmospheric processes.
  • Astronomy: Solar system, stars, galaxies, and space phenomena.
  • Natural Resources and Environment: Renewable and non-renewable resources, environmental conservation.

Practice Tip: Study diagrams of the solar system, Earth layers, and climate patterns.

Scientific Reasoning

Scientific Reasoning tests your ability to think like a scientist, interpret data, and make evidence-based conclusions. Key skills include:

  • Interpreting experiments, tables, and graphs.
  • Analyzing results and identifying patterns or trends.
  • Making predictions based on observations.
  • Drawing logical conclusions using evidence.

Practice Tip: Review sample experiments and practice answering questions about results and conclusions.

Summary: The GED Science section combines knowledge, reasoning, and problem-solving. Focus on understanding key concepts, interpreting data, and applying scientific thinking to succeed.

Social Studies

The GED Social Studies section tests your ability to understand history, civics, economics, geography, and apply critical thinking to real-world situations. It focuses on analyzing information and interpreting data from multiple sources.

History

This includes both US and world history. Key skills:

  • US History: Founding of the nation, the Constitution, civil war, Reconstruction, civil rights movement, and modern history.
  • World History: Major civilizations, events, revolutions, and global conflicts that shaped societies.

Practice Tip: Create timelines of important events and summarize causes and effects of major historical developments.

Civics and Government

This part tests knowledge of government structure, laws, and citizenship. Key skills:

  • Understanding the three branches of government (executive, legislative, judicial).
  • Rights and responsibilities of citizens.
  • Principles of democracy and rule of law.
  • Understanding how policies and laws impact society.

Practice Tip: Study the Constitution, Bill of Rights, and examples of how government functions in daily life.

Economics

Economics focuses on how resources are used, markets operate, and individuals make financial decisions. Key skills:

  • Understanding supply and demand, scarcity, and opportunity cost.
  • Recognizing different types of markets and trade systems.
  • Basic personal finance: budgeting, saving, and investing.
  • Analyzing economic principles in historical and modern contexts.

Practice Tip: Solve real-world problems involving budgeting or interpreting economic data from charts.

Geography

Geography examines the relationship between people and their environment. Key skills:

  • Reading and interpreting maps, globes, and spatial data.
  • Understanding regions, population distribution, and cultural landscapes.
  • Climate and environmental factors that influence human activity.
  • Human-environment interactions, such as urbanization, agriculture, and resource use.

Practice Tip: Practice identifying countries, regions, and physical features on maps, and understand their influence on history and culture.

Critical Thinking

Critical Thinking tests your ability to analyze information, evaluate arguments, and draw conclusions. Key skills:

  • Analyzing primary and secondary sources like documents, charts, and images.
  • Identifying bias, perspective, and reliability of sources.
  • Making evidence-based conclusions and supporting arguments with facts.
  • Comparing historical events or policies and evaluating their outcomes.

Practice Tip: Practice reading historical documents or articles and answering questions about the author’s purpose and evidence used.

Summary: The GED Social Studies section combines knowledge of history, government, economics, and geography with reasoning and critical thinking. Focus on understanding concepts, interpreting sources, and applying evidence to answer questions effectively.
